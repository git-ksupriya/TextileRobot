Hardware setup and working

Hardware Components

1. Servo Motors

The robotic arm uses four servo motors to control the movement of each joint. Servo motors allow precise control of angular position using PWM signals. Each servo contains a DC motor, gearbox, control circuit, and potentiometer that provides position feedback to maintain the desired angle.

Servo Roles

  • Servo 1 – Base Servo: Rotates the entire arm in the horizontal plane.
  • Servo 2 – Arm Servo: Controls forward and backward arm extension.
  • Servo 3 – Vertical Servo: Moves the arm up and down for dipping and stamping.
  • Servo 4 – Orientation Servo: Keeps the sponge facing downward during painting.

2. Raspberry Pi Pico

The Raspberry Pi Pico acts as the central controller of the robotic system. It generates PWM signals to control the servo motors and processes input signals from the color sensor.

  • 26 GPIO pins
  • Supports PWM control
  • Low power consumption
  • Compact and low cost

3. Colour Sensor (TCS3200 / TCS230)

The colour sensor is used to detect the colour of the cloth before painting. It measures the intensity of red, green, and blue light reflected from the surface.

The sensor contains photodiodes with RGB filters. It converts the detected light intensity into frequency signals, which are processed by the microcontroller to determine the dominant colour.

4. End Effector (Sponge Tool)

A sponge is used as the end effector for painting. The sponge has a predefined shape that allows uniform stamping patterns on the cloth. This simple design avoids the need for complex grippers while still achieving repeatable textile patterns.

5. Power Supply

The system is powered using a 5V power supply capable of providing sufficient current to operate the Raspberry Pi Pico and the servo motors. Stable power supply is important to ensure smooth servo motion and reliable sensor readings.
